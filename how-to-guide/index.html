How to Write a Letter to an Official

In this guide, you'll learn how to write a letter or an email to a government official.

Outline

  1. Research
  2. Find Official
  3. Choose Message
  4. Begin Letter
  5. Be Respectful
  6. Use Evidence
  7. Explain
  8. Revise and Send
Step 1

Find an Issue and Research It

What issue do you care about? Make sure to research it properly to make your letter stronger. Take some notes of important details or surprising facts that'll make your letter sound credible and thorough.

Example

For my examples, I'll be focusing on healthcare privatization in Ontario.

Some Topics you Might be Interested in

  • Climate Change
  • Unemployment
  • Healthcare Privatization
Step 2

Find the Appropriate Official

Who will you be writing to? Consider which ministry is most relevant for your concern. You could also to write to your local MP or MPP.

Example

If you're writing about healthcare, you could write to Ontario's Minister of Health or your local MPP.

Step 3

Decide on your main message

Every letter should have a main message. What's the main thing you're concerned about? What angle or perspective do you have on it?

Example

On the topic of healthcare privatization, I could be letting my MPP know that it's highly efficient. I could then bring up points such as reduced wait times and greater capacity. I could alternatively take the opposite viewpoint and base my letter on its inequities. In this case, I would discuss wealth gaps and doctors moving to the private sector ("Ontario is underfunding public healthcare").

Step 4

Begin Your Letter

If you're sending a physical letter, include a heading (see the example). If you're mailing by email, make sure your subject line is concise and references the specific issue (see the examples). Start your letter with a salutation, as you're addressing a dignitary (Ontario.ca). For the letter's body, make sure to start by mentioning who you are, where you live, and how the concern is affecting you. Be sure to talk about what you want the official to do and how you want them to do it.

Example

Here's what a heading and salutation should look like:

June 16, 2025
Honourable Sylvia Jones
Minister of Health
5th Floor
777 Bay St.
Toronto, ON M7A 2J3
By email: sylvia.jones@ontario.ca

Dear Minister Jones,
I am writing to...
Example email subject lines to sylvia.jones@ontario.ca:
  • "Constituent perspective: Support for private delivery options to reduce wait times"
  • "Call to Action: Enforce the Canada Health Act in Ontario"

Step 5

Use Respectful Language

Don't aggressively support or tear something down. Make sure to word your letter calmly and pragmatically, while still getting your point and opinion across. (mention why you need to write respectfully and add source)

Example

Even if I really support or really hate healthcare privatization, I must ensure that my letter respects all viewpoints and politely delivers a message. I should also ensure that I use professional language and avoid words like "obviously" or "as I previously stated" (Danzinger).

Useful Resources

  • Writing letters professionally
  • How to write respectfully (Danzinger, Elizabeth)
  • Formal vs Informal Writing (when writing to an official, you should try to write formally)
Step 6

Support Your Message with Evidence

To ensure your letter has substance, include details and statistics you found from your research. Don't overuse these numbers as they can be dense to read through, but try to include a couple.

Example

In a letter on healthcare privatization, I could talk about the Canada Health Act of 1984, which is Canada's legislature for publicly funded healthcare insurance (Legreid). I could also discuss stakeholders and specific organizations involved in this civic issue. One example I could mention is the Ontario Health Coalition and the protest they led in Queens Park (Carmichael).

Resources I Used

  • Canada Health Act of 1984 (Legreid, Ann M.)
  • Ontario Health Coalition Website
  • Ontario Health Coalition Queens Park Protests (Carmichael, Marney)
Step 7

Explain Your Points

You've got a main message and you've got evidence to back up your viewpoint. Make sure that you adequately connect your evidence back to your angle. If you haven't already, mention what you want the official to do and how they should do it. Otherwise, your letter may come off as a long complaint.

Example

When it comes to healthcare privatization, mentioning scattered protests or laws isn't enough on its own. I would need to explain what these protests say about healthcare providers' stances and how privatization may infringe upon the Canada Health Act.

Step 8

Revise and Send

Read over your letter and make a couple tweaks where needed. Use a sign-off like "Sincerely" or "Respectfully". If you're sending physical mail, be sure to include a return address to give the recipient the option to reply. If you're sending it by email, include some information at the bottom about you, with your full mailing address, phone number, and email address directly beneath your name.

Example

By including a mailing address in the signature block of my email, the government official knows that they are serving someone within their riding/province.
